# Discriminating Tests

This table separates Orch-OR-compatible predictions from nearby alternatives.

| Phenomenon | Orch-OR prediction | Non-OR quantum biology prediction | Classical neurobiology prediction | Discriminating test |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Microtubule exciton transport under anesthetic | Anesthetics reduce transport and alter a coherence-relevant window | Anesthetics alter transport without implying OR-linked collapse timing | Anesthetics alter protein dynamics indirectly or not at all | Compare exciton diffusion with and without anesthetic across matched microtubule states |
| Frequency-sensitive perturbation | Transport and oscillatory response vary across the 20-60 Hz window and anesthetic state | Frequency response is transport-level photophysics only | No coherent frequency-sensitive structure beyond bulk chemistry | Compare microtubule diffusion / resistance / fluorescence before and after perturbation in the 20-60 Hz band |
| UV tryptophan superradiance | Collective UV states may support a wider coherence window | Superradiance is photophysics with no consciousness implication | Fluorescence changes are biochemical/structural only | Measure disorder dependence, lifetime, and quantum yield across architectures |
| Collapse timing | `tau ~ hbar / E_G` predicts a collapse window | Quantum biology may have coherence but no gravity-linked collapse | No special collapse window beyond classical noise | Compare timing against independent decoherence and environmental noise bounds |
| Anesthesia onset | Microtubule-state perturbation contributes to loss of consciousness | Molecular optical/energetic perturbation occurs without Orch-OR collapse | Membrane/receptor mechanisms dominate | Test whether microtubule perturbation changes unconsciousness onset after controlling receptor effects |

The point is not to declare a winner from the table. The point is to make the discriminating experiment obvious.
